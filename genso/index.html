What's this?

You may have learned about the periodic table of elements in school science classes. It is a table that organizes various elements based on their chemical properties in an orderly manner. The GENSOGAKUSHO (© Agedoridori) project aims to anthropomorphize these elements and produce illustrations that depict the world in which they live. The project collaborates with a scientific researcher (N. Nishimura) in the field of nucleosynthesis in the universe, resulting in the GENSOGAKUSHO x GENSOGSEI (nucleosynthesis) project. The illustrations portray anthropomorphic elemental characters engaged in the process of nucleosynthesis in the universe, based on the latest research findings on celestial phenomena and nuclear transformations.

The illustrations show the charm of nucleosynthesis in the universe, presented in a cute characters and sence of humor, in the context of the GENSOGAKUSHO story. This world weaves together the discovery of elements and their chemical properties, and the illustrations of the elements can also serve for authentic scientific commentary.

Interested individuals are invited to explore the GENSOGAKUSHO website and view the presentation slides from the UKAKUREN conference (July 2022).
